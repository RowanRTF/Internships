Step by Step

How to land and complete your internship

From your first conversation to your final portfolio — here's the full roadmap.

01

Define your goals and constraints

Where do you want to work — Philadelphia, South Jersey, NYC? What field? Film production, news, audio, social media? Be honest about your schedule and transportation. These decisions shape everything else.

02

Update your resume and reel

Your resume should highlight your strongest projects, technical skills (Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, After Effects, Avid), and relevant coursework. The Edelman College Center for Professional Success offers resume support and feedback.

03

Talk to the RTF Internship Coordinator

Before you search, make an appointment. The coordinator can confirm your eligibility, connect you with companies that have expressed interest in Rowan students, and help you avoid common mistakes. Reach out to nicolae@rowan.edu.

04

Search and apply

Use this hub, LinkedIn, Handshake, and the company websites listed here. Talk to seniors who have interned. Research the company before applying — know what they make and why you want to work there.

05

Nail your interview

Many internships involve multiple rounds. Be professional, be specific about your skills, and come with two or three portfolio examples ready to discuss. Remember — you are representing Rowan RTF.

06

Register for academic credit

Once you have an accepted offer, complete the Application Form and submit it to the coordinator. They will help you enroll in the internship course. Do not start your internship before completing this step.

Am I Eligible?

Prerequisites & Requirements

You must satisfy these requirements before your internship can be registered for academic credit. Check in with your advisor if you're unsure where you stand.

General Requirements

  • Active RTF major at Rowan University
  • 75 or more earned credit hours
  • Minimum 2.5 GPA overall
  • Accepted an internship offer from an approved organization
  • Completed and submitted the Internship Approval Form

Production Specialization Track

  • Applied Media Aesthetics (completed or currently enrolled)
  • Film Production I (completed or currently enrolled)
  • Television Production (completed or currently enrolled)
  • Foundations of Media Production

Critical Studies Track

  • College Composition I
  • College Composition II
  • Applied Media Aesthetics
  • Non-production internship site approved by coordinator

Credit Options

  • 3 credits = 120 internship hours (one semester)
  • 6 credits = 240 internship hours (full semester or two terms)
  • Summer, fall, and spring semesters all eligible
  • Remote and hybrid internships may qualify — ask coordinator
Exception policy: In rare cases, internships may be approved without all prerequisites with sign-off from your Faculty Advisor, the Internship Coordinator, and the internship site. Reach out early if you have questions about your eligibility — don't assume you're not ready.
For Academic Credit

What's required to earn your grade

Your internship cohort does not meet as a class, but you have four graded components beyond your on-site work.

Component Weight Description
Daily Log 10% A typed, detailed daily log covering all 120 or 240 hours of your internship. Record what you did, learned, and observed each day.
Reflection Paper 30% A 4–5 page paper describing your internship in detail: your experience, the value of the placement, skills developed, and honest thoughts on how the experience could improve.
Portfolio 20% A portfolio of work produced during your internship — video, audio, scripts, web pages, social posts, or other deliverables. Examples are available from the coordinator.
Supervisor Evaluation 40% Your internship supervisor will be contacted for a confidential evaluation of your performance. This is the highest-weighted component — your professionalism at the internship site matters.
